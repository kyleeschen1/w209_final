Do Democrats or Republican drop out more in non-Presidential election years?


Bill Clinton infamously mused that "Democrats fall in love, and Republicans fall in line." He believed that Democrats need a charismatic vision to bring them to the polls, while Republicans will participate regardless of their enthusiasm.

If Clinton is right, then Democratic Congressional turnout would likely increase in presidential election years (as those "charismatic visions" are more likely to be voiced), while Republicans will be comparatively consistent cycle to cycle.

Let's test if this. Each circle shows the average votes cast for each party in each state for all House and Senate elections over 2004-2018.
